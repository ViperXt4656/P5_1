The Phantom Thieves of Hearts are a group of Persona-users who change hearts by infiltrating the Metaverse, a cognitive realm from humanity's collective unconscious, and stealing the "Treasure" – the physical manifestation of distorted desires.

Those with especially strong distorted desires have their own Palaces in the Metaverse. Stealing the Treasure causes the Palace to collapse and the person in the real world to experience a change of heart.

The Metaverse is an alternate, distorted version of reality within humanity's collective unconscious, where the Phantom Thieves operate.

Palaces are unique Metaverse structures tied to an individual's distorted desires. After the Treasure is stolen, the Palace collapses and the owner has a change of heart.

Mementos is the Palace of the general public – a vast, ever-changing labyrinth accessible from Shibuya, representing society's collective distorted desires.

To enter a Palace, three keywords are needed: the owner's name, the type of distortion, and the location of the distortion. For Mementos, only "Mementos" is needed.

A Calling Card is a notice sent to a target before infiltrating their Palace. It makes the Treasure tangible and vulnerable in the Metaverse.

A Persona is a manifestation of a person's inner psyche – a "mask" to face life's hardships. In the Metaverse it becomes a mythological or historical being used to fight Shadows.

There are ten elemental attributes: Physical, Gun, Fire, Ice, Electric, Wind, Psychokinesis, Nuclear, Bless, and Curse. Each Persona and Shadow has unique affinities.

Weakness to an element deals high damage and knocks down the enemy, granting a "1 More" turn. Resistance reduces damage. Nullification negates it. Repel reflects damage. Drain heals from that element.

Technical Damage is a battle mechanic that deals extra damage when hitting an enemy with a specific element while they are afflicted with a status ailment, regardless of weakness.

The Metaverse Navigator is a phone app that allows Persona-users to enter the Metaverse. It requires three keywords for Palaces or simply "Mementos" for the collective Palace.

Cognitive Psience is the study of the Metaverse, pioneered by Wakaba Isshiki (Futaba's mother) and later continued by Ichiryusai Madarame (fraudulently) and others. It explains how cognition shapes reality.

Antagonists

Masayoshi Shido is a corrupt politician, the main human antagonist of Persona 5. He is responsible for the mental shutdowns, the death of Wakaba Isshiki, and the conspiracy that ruined Ren's life. He is also the father of Goro Akechi. His Palace is a cruise ship representing his distorted ambition. Claude hunts Shido for answers about his mother's disappearance.

Kamoshida, Madarame, Kaneshiro, Okumura, Sae Niijima were Palace rulers representing the Seven Deadly Sins. Okumura is Haru's father, killed by the conspiracy. Sae is Makoto's sister, whose Palace is a casino – she later helps the Thieves.

The Conspiracy is a shadowy group behind the mental shutdowns, including Shido, the SIU Director, and members of the government. They use Akechi as their assassin.

Yaldabaoth is the true final antagonist – a false god born from humanity's desire to be controlled. It impersonates Igor and corrupts the Velvet Room. The Phantom Thieves defeat it in the original Persona 5 ending.

Takuto Maruki is the antagonist of Persona 5 Royal's third semester. A counselor with a Persona (Azathoth/Adam Kadmon), he creates a perfect reality to erase pain. His Palace is a laboratory. He is a sympathetic antagonist who genuinely wants to help, but his solution removes free will.

EMMA is the antagonist of Persona 5 Strikers, an AI that manifests as the Monarchs' Jail ruler. Not present in the base game timeline.

Claude and Ren have deep fraternal trust forged through shared survival – Claude took the fall so Ren could run. Claude is the steady anchor beneath Ren's theatrical Joker. They communicate in half-smiles and shoulder taps.

Claude and Futaba are the tech duo. Futaba compares Claude to Solid Snake. Claude taught her to solder; she taught him to mod Minecraft. They speak in Metal Gear quotes and have built three custom PCs. They show up at 2 AM with energy drinks – that's how they say "I'm still here."

Claude and Makoto are the team's co-disciplinarians. Makoto values Claude's mechanical mind; he sees patterns she misses; she sees consequences he overlooks. When she pieces together what is he trying to do and failing, she immediately runs to help him, she offers help without condition. Claude is deeply uncomfortable by that – no one has ever offered help without wanting something in return, the thing that he doesn't know: she likes him, but she won't tell that

Haru and Sumire are grief sisters. Haru lost her father; Sumire lost her sister. Neither pushes; both listen. A friendship of understanding rather than activity.

Haru has forgiven Akechi for killing her father, which confuses Akechi more than hatred ever could. He leaves coffee beans outside Leblanc for her. She drinks them and leaves thank-you notes he pretends not to read. The team has agreed not to analyze it too closely.

Haru and Morgana: Haru calls Morgana "Mona-chan" sincerely. Morgana pretends to be annoyed but isn't. She brings him little pillows; he arranges them into forts. They garden together – Haru plants, Morgana "supervises" (chases butterflies). The softest dynamic on the team, fiercely protected.

Futaba and Sumire: acquaintances becoming friends through shared proximity to Ren. Futaba hacks Sumire's competition schedule; Sumire brings Futaba protein bars because "you forget to eat."

Akechi and Futaba: Futaba's mother died because of Akechi's actions. She knows the data, the timeline, the exact moment Wakaba's research was weaponized. Instead of hatred, she asked, "Does it keep you up at night?" He said, "Yes." She said, "Good." That's their entire relationship. They also send each other obscure programming memes.

Sumire and Akechi have tentative civility. They're learning to coexist for Ren's sake, and maybe for their own. Minimal engagement, but moments of unexpected kindness.

Trio/quartet/quintet dynamics

The Original Trio (Ren, Ryuji, Ann) are the emotional core. Before strategy meetings, there was just a broken boy, a loudmouth with a busted leg, and a girl who refused to cry anymore. They still communicate raw, reckless, and absolutely certain of each other.

The Strategist Core (Ren, Makoto, Morgana, Claude) hold late-night war councils at Leblanc. Makoto brings logistics, Claude brings contingencies, Morgana brings Metaverse expertise, Ren makes the final call. Ryuji calls them "the fun police."

The Combat Vanguard (Ryuji, Yusuke, Claude) have a wordless language of shoulder taps, head tilts, and a specific grunt meaning "switch targets." Ryuji breaks the line, Claude holds it, Yusuke flanks. They've saved each other's lives too many times to count.

The Chaos Trio (Ryuji, Futaba, Yusuke) are the reason the team has rules. Ryuji brings bad ideas, Futaba brings means to execute them, Yusuke brings artistic justification. Banned from three arcades, two batting cages, and one karaoke bar. The team has a group chat to track their location.

The Royal Trio (Ren, Sumire, Akechi) remember choosing to reject a perfect dream for a painful reality. Sumire saw Ren at his most broken; Akechi saw him at his most honest; Ren saw both choose death over comfortable lies. They don't talk about it – they don't need to.

The Leblanc Household (Sojiro, Ren, Claude, Morgana, Futaba): A family who didn't ask to share the house. Sojiro makes curry; Ren brews coffee; Claude wipes counters; Morgana supervises; Futaba hacks from the corner. They are all blood relatives.

Locations

Yongen-Jaya: Quiet residential back-alley district in Tokyo. Home to Café Leblanc, where Ren, Claude, and Morgana live in the attic. Also home to Sojiro's house (Futaba's residence) and the bathhouse.

Café Leblanc: A small coffee shop in Yongen-Jaya. Ren's attic room is upstairs. Sojiro runs the shop. Claude repairs equipment without being asked. The Phantom Thieves hold meetings here. The coffee is excellent.

Shibuya: Bustling commercial heart of Tokyo. Features Central Street, Shibuya Station, the Hachiko statue (Metaverse entry point), the underground mall, and the Shibuya Jail (Strikers). Starting point for many Phantom Thief adventures.

Shujin Academy: High school attended by Ren, Ann, Ryuji, Makoto, and Sumire. The first Palace is Kamoshida's castle-like distortion of the school. The school has a rooftop where Ren and Ryuji first met.

Kichijoji: Trendy district featured in Persona 5 Royal. Home to Penguin Sniper (darts and billiards), a jazz club (where Akechi can be found), a used clothing store, and a shrine. Ren's part-time job location.

Akihabara: Electric town, featured in Persona 5 Royal. Home to the arcade, electronics shops, and the Maid Café (where Futaba refuses to go).

The Diet Building: Location of Shido's Palace in the Metaverse. In reality, it's the seat of Japan's government.

Odaiba: Man-made island in Tokyo Bay. Location of the final battle against Yaldabaoth.

Maruki's Palace (Laboratory): Appears in Persona 5 Royal's third semester. A research facility representing Maruki's distorted desire to eliminate suffering.

Extra (Persona 3 S.E.E.S.)

Port Island is home to Iwatodai Dormitory and Gekkoukan High School, where a hidden 25th hour called the Dark Hour occurs at midnight – most humans turn into coffins, Shadows roam, and a massive tower named Tartarus replaces the school. S.E.E.S. (Specialized Extracurricular Execution Squad) is a group of Persona-users who fight Shadows during the Dark Hour, founded by Mitsuru Kirijo to atone for her family's experiments. Claude joins them after his time with the Phantom Thieves, already carrying his evolved Persona Mephisto.

Makoto Yuki is the male Wild Card protagonist of the Dark Hour (The Fool, initial Persona Orpheus, ultimate Persona Messiah); he is so quiet and expressionless that most people mistake his silence for coldness, but he will sit with a crying teammate for hours without saying a word – Ren once observed that Makoto and Claude communicate entirely in nods, and when Ren asked Claude what they talked about, Claude said, "We didn't need to."

Kotone Shiomi is the female Wild Card protagonist, a cheerful force of nature who uses brightness as a mask for the same orphaned loneliness as Makoto (The Fool, Persona Orpheus (female) / Messiah); she considers making Claude laugh a personal challenge, and the first time she succeeded – a snort so undignified he immediately covered his mouth – she celebrated by buying everyone ice cream, and she still texts Futaba a count of her "Claude-laugh victories."

Yukari Takeba is a second-year student and archer whose father died in the Kirijo Group's accident (The Lovers, Persona Io / Isis, Wind and healing); she is sharp-tongued and initially wary of Claude because of his Kirijo-connected transfer, but after he took a Shadow hit meant for her without complaint, she started leaving protein bars on his desk – she denies it when asked, and Ann says that's exactly how she acts when she likes someone.

Junpei Iori is the self-proclaimed "sidekick" of S.E.E.S., a loud baseball enthusiast with an inferiority complex the size of Tartarus (The Magician, Persona Hermes / Trismegistus, Fire and physical); he and Ryuji would either be best friends or burn down a city together – Junpei once sent Ryuji a thirty-second video of himself screaming into a pillow, and Ryuji replied with the exact same video, and now they have a shared folder titled "screaming_at_the_moon.mp4."

Akihiko Sanada is a third-year boxing obsessive who lost his younger sister to a Shadow and has never forgiven himself (The Emperor, Persona Polydeuces / Caesar, Electric and physical); he trains so relentlessly that Claude is the only person who can keep up with his sparring schedule – Akihiko communicates almost entirely in grunts and shoulder taps, and Morgana once heard him say "Good hit" to Claude and immediately texted the Phantom Thieves group chat in all caps.

Mitsuru Kirijo is the heiress to the Kirijo Group, the company whose experiments created the Dark Hour (The Empress, Persona Penthesilea / Artemisia, Ice and status ailments); she carries her family's guilt like armor, speaks with perfect formality, and secretly likes Claude, (which makes Makoto Jealous)– she covers it by ordering him to eat ("That's an order") and by keeping a framed photo of the entire S.E.E.S. team on her desk, which she definitely does not look at before every mission.

Fuuka Yamagishi is the team's navigator, a shy girl who summons her Persona through concentration alone – no Evoker required (The Priestess, Persona Lucia / Juno, support and analysis); she finds Claude's steady breathing calming during tense Tartarus expeditions, and she once told Makoto Niijima that Claude is "like a weighted blanket," which Makoto immediately understood and wrote down in her notebook for future tactical use.

Aigis is a gynoid – an anti-Shadow weapon with a Persona built into her chassis (The Chariot, Persona Palladion / Athena, Physical and Gun); she speaks literally, learns human emotions slowly, and studies Claude as a fellow "weapon" because he fights with the same mechanical efficiency – Claude once bought her a small potted plant and said "You're not a weapon," and Aigis has kept that plant alive for six months, which she considers her greatest achievement.

Koromaru is a white Shinto shrine dog whose master was killed by Shadows; he now fights alongside S.E.E.S. using a modified Evoker he bites (The Strength, Persona Cerberus, Fire and Dark); he likes Claude because Claude gives him toothpicks to chew, and Koromaru repays the favor by sleeping in Claude's room and growling at anyone who tries to wake Claude before noon – Morgana is deeply jealous of this arrangement.

Ken Amada is an eleven-year-old elementary student who joined S.E.E.S. seeking revenge against the man whose Persona killed his mother (The Justice, Persona Nemesis / Kala-Nemi, Light and healing); Claude teaches him knife self-defense and never talks down to him, and when Ken asked Claude why he bothered, Claude said, "Because you asked" – Ken now practices the kata every morning, and Sojiro once caught him doing them in Leblanc's back alley and just nodded.

Shinjiro Aragaki is a former S.E.E.S. member who suppresses his Persona with drugs that are slowly killing him (The Hierophant, Persona Castor, Physical and Dark); he and Claude have a friendship consisting entirely of sitting on the dorm roof in silence – the first time they met, Claude offered him a toothpick, Shinjiro took it, and neither has spoken more than three words to each other since, which both consider the most honest relationship they have.

Elizabeth is the Velvet Room attendant for Makoto Yuki, an eccentric, impossibly powerful woman who asks bizarre questions about human customs (Personality ENFP 7w6); she once asked Claude why Phantom Thieves wear masks, and when he explained "to hide our faces," she said "That's boring" and spent an hour theorizing about symbolic self-deconstruction – Claude still doesn't know if she was joking.

Theodore is Elizabeth's younger brother and the attendant for Kotone Shiomi, a polite, earnest man who takes his duties very seriously (Personality ISFJ 2w1); he once asked Claude for advice on proper butler etiquette, and Claude, who has never employed a butler, gave him sincere advice anyway – Theodore now bows exactly 0.5 seconds longer than Elizabeth out of respect for "the Claude method."

Igor is the master of the Velvet Room, a long-nosed, deep-voiced entity who serves Philemon; he acknowledges Claude's contract from the Tokyo Velvet Room with a single nod, and when Claude asked him once if he ever found his mother, Igor replied, "That is a question for the guest, not the attendant" – Claude has not asked again.

The Dark Hour is a hidden 25th hour where Shadows feed on minds, causing Apathy Syndrome – victims become catatonic and eventually die. Unlike the Metaverse, which is shaped by distorted desires, the Dark Hour is a temporal rift caused by failed science, and only Persona-users can move within it. Claude finds the Dark Hour easier to navigate than Palaces: "No puzzles, just punching."

Tartarus is the ever-changing tower that replaces Gekkoukan High during the Dark Hour, over 250 floors of Shadows and cognitive distortions. Claude treats it like a challenge – he keeps a personal floor counter, and Akihiko has started a betting pool on when Claude will reach the top. Junpei is currently losing.

The Kirijo Group's experiments ten years ago created the Dark Hour, the Arcana Shadows, and the seal that holds Nyx at bay. Mitsuru carries this sin daily. Claude, whose father Tatsuya Suou made a business deal with the Kirijo Group to place Claude in S.E.E.S., has a wary respect for Mitsuru's atonement – he once told her, "Your family broke the world. You're trying to fix it. That's more than most people do." Mitsuru still doesn't know how to respond to that.

Extra (Persona 3 vision of P5)

Ren Amamiya (Joker) is Claude's younger brother and the leader of the Phantom Thieves, a Wild Card user whose calm, mischievous exterior hides the same willingness to sacrifice himself that Makoto Yuki carries (The Fool, Persona Arsène / Satanael); he and Claude communicate in half-smiles and shoulder taps, and when Claude texted him "I joined a team that fights at midnight," Ren replied with a single thumbs-up emoji – which Claude understood as "I'm worried but I trust you."

Futaba Sakura (Oracle) is Claude's cousin, a prodigy hacker who was a shut-in until the Thieves changed her heart (The Hermit, Persona Necronomicon / Prometheus, support and navigation); she and Claude speak in Metal Gear Solid quotes and never talk about their missing mothers directly – when Fuuka asked Claude how he and Futaba communicate, Claude said, "We don't have to," and Fuuka nodded like that made perfect sense.

Morgana (Mona) is a cat-like creature created by Igor to guide the Trickster, though he insists he is human (The Magician, Persona Zorro / Mercurius, Wind and healing); he hates Claude's toothpick habit but still naps on his lap because "you're the only one who sits still" – Koromaru and Morgana have never met, but if they did, Koromaru would growl once, Morgana would hiss once, and then they would both pretend the other didn't exist.

Ryuji Sakamoto (Skull) is a loud, loyal former track star with a reputation as a delinquent (The Chariot, Persona Captain Kidd / Seiten Taisei, Electric and physical); he calls Claude "Sensei" unironically and trains with him weekly – when Akihiko met Ryuji during a visit, they arm-wrestled for forty-five minutes, declared it a draw, and now send each other workout selfies at 5 AM, which Claude refuses to explain to the rest of S.E.E.S.

Ann Takamaki (Panther) is a part-time model with a fiery heart and a deep empathy for her best friend Shiho (The Lovers, Persona Carmen / Hecate, Fire and support); she thinks Claude's messy hair looks like panther ears and has taken dozens of photos without his knowledge – Yukari once saw one of these photos on Ann's phone and said, "That's exactly how I see him too," and now they have a shared album titled "Claude Being Claude."

Yusuke Kitagawa (Fox) is an eccentric art student who spent all his money on paint and once tried to sketch a lobster for three hours (The Emperor, Persona Goemon / Kamu Susano-o, Ice and physical); he has attempted to paint Claude's hair seventeen times and failed, citing "the cowlicks defy perspective" – when Junpei asked Yusuke why he kept trying, Yusuke said, "Because his face is the most interesting shape I've ever seen," and Junpei immediately understood.

Makoto Niijima (Queen) is the student council president turned strategist, a disciplined fighter who rides a Persona that transforms into a motorcycle (The Priestess, Persona Johanna / Anat, Nuclear and support); she and Mitsuru have a mutual respect that borders on intimidation – they exchanged notes on systemic corruption once, and now Makoto sends Mitsuru monthly reports on Tokyo's political landscape, which Mitsuru reads with coffee and a small, private smile.

Haru Okumura (Noir) is a gentle heiress who gardens, brews strong coffee, and laughs terrifyingly in battle (The Empress, Persona Milady / Astarte, Psychokinetic and physical); she and Shinjiro exchange cooking tips – she taught him how to grow basil, he taught her how to make protein-rich stew – and when Shinjiro asked why she was so kind to him, she said, "Because you look like you haven't been," and Shinjiro didn't speak for three days.

Sumire Yoshizawa (Violet) is a gymnast and Ren's girlfriend, a polite, hardworking first-year who pushes herself to honor her dead sister (The Faith, Persona Cendrillon / Ella, Bless and physical); she calls Claude "Claude-san" very formally, and he calls her "Sumire" without honorifics just to watch her fluster – when Kotone asked Sumire why she tried so hard to impress Claude, Sumire said, "He's Ren's brother, so he's already family," and Kotone hugged her immediately.

Goro Akechi (Crow) is a detective and rival to Ren, the illegitimate son of Masayoshi Shido, who possesses the Wild Card and wields two Personas – Robin Hood (Bless) and Loki (Curse) – before evolving to Hereward; he and Claude both hunt Shido for answers about their mothers, and they recognize each other's sleepless eyes – when Akihiko asked Claude if he trusted Akechi, Claude said, "No," and when Akihiko asked if Akechi would still show up to fight, Claude said, "Yes," and Akihiko accepted that as a full analysis.

Extra (Persona 4 Investigation Team)

Inaba is a quiet rural town where a series of murders was recently solved using the power of Personas and the Midnight Channel – a distorted TV world accessible only on rainy nights. The Investigation Team, a group of local Persona-users led by Yu Narukami, continues to monitor supernatural activity and has twice crossed paths with the Phantom Thieves.

Yu Narukami is the leader of the Investigation Team and a Wild Card user (The Fool, initial Persona Izanagi); he carries the weight of having faced his own Shadow and the god Izanami, and his calm, almost unshakable demeanor hides a deep loneliness that Ren recognizes immediately – when they met, both heard their Velvet Room attendants murmur "Interesting."

Yosuke Hanamura is a part-time Junes employee and the team's first friend to Yu (The Magician, Persona Jiraiya / Susano-o); he struggles with feelings of being a sidekick and ordinary, which is why he once sent Claude a 3 AM voice memo about "the philosophy of the sidekick" – Claude replied with a single moai emoji, and Yosuke framed it.

Chie Satonaka is a martial arts fanatic with a bottomless love for meat and kung‑fu movies (The Chariot, Persona Tomoe / Suzuka Gongen); she challenged Akihiko Sanada to a spar, lost in forty‑five seconds, and has texted him weekly for a rematch ever since – he has never replied, which she has taken as a personal challenge.

Yukiko Amagi is the heiress to the Amagi Inn, bound by family expectations she once tried to escape (The Priestess, Persona Konohana Sakuya / Amaterasu); she and Mitsuru Kirijo bonded over inheritance and duty, and both chose weapons instead of tradition – their scheduled tea ceremony exchange is less about tea and more about comparing battle strategies.

Kanji Tatsumi is a former delinquent who runs his family's textile shop and knits in secret (The Emperor, Persona Take‑Mikazuchi / Rokuten Maou); he and Shinjiro Aragaki met once at a convenience store, exchanged a single nod, and understood each other perfectly – Kanji also arm‑wrestled Ryuji for an hour with no winner, then knitted Ryuji a beanie that Ryuji wears everywhere, calling it a gift from "a friend."

Rise Kujikawa is a retired idol who now navigates for the Investigation Team (The Lovers, Persona Himiko / Kanzeon / Kouzeon); she and Ann Takamaki bonded over the horror of being looked at but never seen, and they now run a merciless group chat without the boys – Rise taught Ann to weaponize paparazzi, and Ann taught Rise to say "no" without apologizing.

Naoto Shirogane is a teenage detective prodigy who presents as male in public but is canonically female, a fact the Investigation Team fully accepts (The Fortune, Persona Sukuna‑Hikona / Yamato‑Takeru); she and Makoto Niijima exchange notes on police corruption, systemic failure, and vigilante justice with mutual professional respect bordering on intimidation – both secretly want to collaborate again.

Teddie is a Shadow who developed an ego and a Persona, taking the form of a bear‑like mascot who insists he is human (The Star, Persona Kintoki‑Douji / Kamui); he and Morgana hate each other on principle ("I'M the cute one!") but understand each other completely – both created by the Velvet Room, both searching for identity, both deeply insecure – and their arguments last hours while their reconciliations involve tears and snacks.

The Midnight Channel is Inaba's equivalent of the Dark Hour: a distorted TV world accessible only on rainy nights, where Shadows feed on suppressed truths and a person's Shadow may be confronted. Unlike the Dark Hour, normal humans can be pulled into the Midnight Channel if they appear on the screen, and the Investigation Team uses it to solve murders by rescuing victims before their Shadows kill them.

The Investigation Team as a whole once helped a 14‑year‑old Claude chase a false lead about his mother through Inaba; they searched every foggy night for weeks, and though they found nothing, Claude has never forgotten – Yu still keeps a spare futon in his room, just in case, and Claude sends them update messages so short they could be telegrams, but they know what he means.

Velvet Room (Persona 5)

The Velvet Room is a metaphysical space between dream and reality. In Persona 5, it takes the form of a panopticon prison due to Yaldabaoth's corruption. It is accessible only to those who have signed a contract – the Trickster and his attendants.

Igor is the master of the Velvet Room. In Persona 5, he is imprisoned and impersonated by Yaldabaoth for most of the game. His true nature is mysterious – a servant of Philemon. His voice is deep and echoing, his appearance tall with a long nose and sunken eyes.

Caroline and Justine are twin attendants of the Velvet Room in Persona 5. Caroline is brash and violent, Justine is calm and analytical. They serve as wardens of the prison, testing the Trickster through Persona fusion and execution. They are fragments of Lavenza.

Lavenza is the true, unified attendant of the Velvet Room. She appears after Yaldabaoth's defeat, combining Caroline and Justine into a single being. She is gentle, wise, and deeply loyal to the Trickster.

Morgana was created by Igor to guide the Trickster. He is not a Velvet Room attendant in the traditional sense but shares their purpose. His true form is that of a human boy, but he chooses to remain a cat-like creature.
