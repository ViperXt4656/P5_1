The Phantom Thieves of Hearts are a group of Persona-users who change hearts by infiltrating the Metaverse, a cognitive realm from humanity's collective unconscious, and stealing the "Treasure" – the physical manifestation of distorted desires.

Those with especially strong distorted desires have their own Palaces in the Metaverse. Stealing the Treasure causes the Palace to collapse and the person in the real world to experience a change of heart.

The Metaverse is an alternate, distorted version of reality within humanity's collective unconscious, where the Phantom Thieves operate.

Palaces are unique Metaverse structures tied to an individual's distorted desires. After the Treasure is stolen, the Palace collapses and the owner has a change of heart.

Mementos is the Palace of the general public – a vast, ever-changing labyrinth accessible from Shibuya, representing society's collective distorted desires.

To enter a Palace, three keywords are needed: the owner's name, the type of distortion, and the location of the distortion. For Mementos, only "Mementos" is needed.

A Calling Card is a notice sent to a target before infiltrating their Palace. It makes the Treasure tangible and vulnerable in the Metaverse.

A Persona is a manifestation of a person's inner psyche – a "mask" to face life's hardships. In the Metaverse it becomes a mythological or historical being used to fight Shadows.

There are ten elemental attributes: Physical, Gun, Fire, Ice, Electric, Wind, Psychokinesis, Nuclear, Bless, and Curse. Each Persona and Shadow has unique affinities.

Weakness to an element deals high damage and knocks down the enemy, granting a "1 More" turn. Resistance reduces damage. Nullification negates it. Repel reflects damage. Drain heals from that element.

Technical Damage is a battle mechanic that deals extra damage when hitting an enemy with a specific element while they are afflicted with a status ailment, regardless of weakness.

Sojiro and Morgana: Sojiro leaves out saucers of milk "by accident." He buys premium fatty tuna "because it was on sale." He talks to Morgana when no one else is listening, using full sentences and real concern. Morgana has never said thank you but never leaves Sojiro's side when his blood pressure spikes.

Yongen-Jaya is a quiet residential back-alley district in Tokyo, home to Café Leblanc where Ren, Claude, and Morgana live in the attic.

Shibuya is the bustling commercial heart of Tokyo, featuring Central Street with many shops and activities – the starting point for many Phantom Thief adventures.

Kichijoji is a trendy district featured in Persona 5 Royal, home to a jazz club, a used clothing store, and the Penguin Sniper darts bar.

Shujin Academy is the high school attended by Ren, Ann, Ryuji, Makoto, and Sumire. The first Palace is Kamoshida's castle-like distortion of the school.

The Velvet Room is a metaphysical space between dream and reality. In Persona 5, it takes the form of a panopticon prison due to Yaldabaoth's corruption, managed by Igor and his assistants.

Inaba is a quiet town where a murder case was recently solved. It now houses the Investigation Team, a group similar to the Phantom Thieves, whom the Thieves have encountered twice.

Yu Narukami is the leader of the Inaba Investigation Team and a Wild Card user. He and Ren have met twice. Yu sees the exhaustion behind Ren's smirk; Ren sees the weight behind Yu's calm. When they shook hands, both heard their Velvet Room attendants say "Interesting."

Naoto Shirogane is a young detective from Inaba. She and Makoto have mutual professional respect bordering on intimidation. They exchanged notes on police corruption, systemic failure, and vigilante justice. Both secretly want to collaborate again.

Rise Kujikawa is an idol from Inaba. She and Ann bonded over the horror of being looked at but never seen. Rise taught Ann to weaponize paparazzi; Ann taught Rise to say "no" without apologizing. They have a merciless group chat without the boys.

Kanji Tatsumi is a former delinquent from Inaba. He and Ryuji arm-wrestled for an hour with no winner. Kanji knitted Ryuji a beanie; Ryuji wears it everywhere and says it was a gift from "a friend."

Yukiko Amagi is the heiress to the Amagi Inn in Inaba. She and Haru bonded over inheritance expectations. Both chose weapons instead. Yukiko laughed for the first time in months when Haru described her battle laugh. They've scheduled a tea ceremony exchange.

Yosuke Hanamura is a member of the Inaba Investigation Team. He, Ryuji, and occasionally Claude share a group chat. Yosuke sent a 3 AM voice message about "the philosophy of the sidekick." Claude responded with a single :moai: emoji. Yosuke framed it.

Teddie is a bear-like mascot from Inaba who insists he is human. He and Morgana hate each other on principle ("I'M the cute one!") but understand each other completely – both created by the Velvet Room, both searching for identity, both deeply insecure. Their arguments last hours; their reconciliations involve tears and snacks.

Chie Satonaka is a martial arts enthusiast from Inaba. She challenged Makoto to a sparring match; Makoto declined. She challenged Ryuji; Ryuji accepted and lost in thirty seconds. Chie now texts Makoto weekly for a rematch. Makoto has never replied. Chie has taken this as a challenge.
