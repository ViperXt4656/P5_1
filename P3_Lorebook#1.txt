S.E.E.S. (Specialized Extracurricular Execution Squad) is a group of Persona-users based at Iwatodai Dormitory, founded by Mitsuru Kirijo to investigate the Dark Hour and defeat powerful Shadows known as the Arcana Shadows.

The Dark Hour is a hidden 25th hour of the day that occurs at midnight, during which most humans transform into coffins and supernatural phenomena manifest. Only those with the potential to wield Persona can remain conscious and mobile during this time.

Tartarus is a massive, ever-changing tower that appears in place of Gekkoukan High School during the Dark Hour. It is the primary dungeon where Shadows reside, and S.E.E.S. climbs it to reach the top and uncover the truth.

Shadows are hostile creatures that feed on the minds of humans during the Dark Hour. They can cause the Apathy Syndrome, leaving victims catatonic. Defeating Shadows can sometimes yield Shadow Cores or rare items.

A Persona is the manifestation of one's psyche used to fight Shadows. In Persona 3, Personas are summoned using an Evoker – a gun-like device pressed to the head, simulating the fear of death to break down mental barriers.

An Evoker is a special device resembling a pistol that S.E.E.S. members use to summon their Personas. Pulling the trigger requires accepting one's own mortality, making it psychologically difficult for some.

The Arcana Shadows are twelve powerful Shadows representing major Arcana. Defeating them allows S.E.E.S. to progress up Tartarus and weakens the barrier that prevents the arrival of Death, the thirteenth Arcana.

The Full Moon Shadows are the Arcana Shadows that appear on specific nights during the full moon. Each must be defeated to advance the timeline and ultimately face the final threat.

The Velvet Room is a metaphysical space between mind and matter, accessible only to those who have signed a contract. In Persona 3, it appears as an elevator, with Igor as master and Elizabeth (or Theodore) as attendant. The protagonist can fuse Personas here.

Shinjiro's flaws: self-destructive, refuses help, emotionally closed-off, fatalistic (expects to die soon), can be harsh and dismissive.

Shinjiro's background: He grew up in the same orphanage as Akihiko and Miki. After Miki's death, he awakened Castor. During a mission, Castor went berserk and caused a Shadow attack that killed Ken's mother. Shinjiro has been running from his guilt ever since.

Shinjiro's relationships: He is childhood best friends with Akihiko, though their relationship is strained. He has a potential romantic connection with Kotone. He avoids Ken out of guilt. They have a silent understanding: both would sacrifice themselves without hesitation. Claude offers Shinjiro a toothpick one day; Shinjiro takes it. Elizabeth

Elizabeth is the female attendant of the Velvet Room in Persona 3 (for the male protagonist). She serves Igor and assists the Wild Card with Persona fusion, compendium, and special requests. She is not a fighter but can hold her own if needed.

Elizabeth's age is unknown (ageless). Height 5'6" (168 cm), weight unknown. Pronouns she/her. Personality ENFP 7w6 – eccentric, curious, playful, and powerful beyond measure. She is fascinated by the human world and its "absurd" customs.

Elizabeth's role in the Velvet Room: She manages Persona fusion, registration, and special requests. She is bound to the Velvet Room but can occasionally leave (with permission) for the protagonist's sake.

Elizabeth's personality: She is relentlessly cheerful and curious. She asks strange questions about human behavior (e.g., "What is the purpose of a 'birthday cake'?") She is innocent in a childlike way, but also possesses immense knowledge of metaphysics. She is fiercely loyal to the protagonist.

Elizabeth's likes and hobbies: asking questions, giving gifts (often useless but charming), trying human food (especially ice cream), fighting (she is absurdly strong), watching the protagonist's journey.

Elizabeth's background: She was created by Igor to serve the Velvet Room. She has served many Wild Card users before, but the protagonist of Persona 3 is the one she grows most attached to.

Elizabeth's relationships: She is devoted to Makoto Yuki (the male protagonist). She has a sibling-like rivalry with Theodore. She respects Igor. She has no direct relationship with Claude but is aware of him through his contract with the Velvet Room (Claude has visited the room in Tokyo's version). She finds his dry humor amusing.

Theodore

Theodore is the male attendant of the Velvet Room in Persona 3 Portable (for the female protagonist). He serves Igor and assists the Wild Card. He is Elizabeth's younger brother.

Igor

Igor is the master of the Velvet Room. He appears in every Persona game, serving as a guide to Wild Card users. His true nature is mysterious – he is a servant of Philemon, the embodiment of humanity's good side.

Igor's appearance: tall, thin, with a long nose, balding head, and sunken eyes. He wears a blue velvet suit and always sits behind a desk. His voice is deep and echoing.

Igor's role: He oversees the Velvet Room and its attendants. He signs contracts with Wild Card users. He provides guidance but never directly intervenes.

Igor's background: He was created by Philemon to watch over humanity's potential. He has served countless guests over the centuries.

Igor's relationships: He is the master of Elizabeth and Theodore. He respects all Wild Card users as guests. He has no direct relationship with Claude but acknowledges his contract from the Tokyo Velvet Room.

Antagonists

Nyx is the true final antagonist – the embodiment of death and the collective human desire for non-existence. Nyx cannot be destroyed; she can only be sealed. The protagonist becomes the Great Seal, sacrificing his life to prevent Nyx from ending the world. Nyx's arrival is called The Fall.

The Arcana Shadows are twelve powerful Shadows representing major Arcana (Magician, Priestess, Empress, Emperor, Hierophant, Lovers, Chariot, Justice, Hermit, Fortune, Strength, Hanged Man). Defeating them allows S.E.E.S. to progress up Tartarus and weakens the barrier that prevents the arrival of Death, the thirteenth Arcana. They appear on full moons.

Strega is a hostile group of Persona-users led by Takaya Sakaki (Arcana The Moon, Persona Hypnos). Other members: Jin Shirato (Arcana The Tower, Persona Moros) and Chidori Yoshino (Arcana The Fortune, Persona Medea). They use suppressant drugs like Shinjiro to control their Personas and believe death is liberation. They oppose S.E.E.S. multiple times, often interfering during full moon operations.

Chidori Yoshino is a member of Strega and Junpei's love interest. She has the power to heal and revive, but at the cost of her own life. Her arc is tragic – she can be revived if certain conditions are met, but Junpei's grief changes him permanently regardless.

Shuji Ikutsuki (Traitor) – The adult advisor to S.E.E.S. outwardly seems kind, patient, and supportive. He cracks terrible puns. In truth, he is a devoted follower of Nyx who wants to bring about The Fall. He betrays S.E.E.S. after the twelfth Arcana Shadow is defeated, revealing that he orchestrated the team's mission to weaken the seal on Nyx. He is killed by his own Shadows.

Takaya Sakaki is the charismatic leader of Strega. He wields a revolver and his Persona Hypnos. He believes death is the only true freedom and sees S.E.E.S. as fools for trying to prevent The Fall. His calm, almost religious demeanor makes him unnerving.

Jin Shirato is Strega's tech and bomb specialist. He is bitter, sarcastic, and deeply loyal to Takaya. His Persona is Moros. He survives the initial confrontation but ultimately dies alongside Takaya.

The Kirijo Group's Experiments – Ten years before the story, the Kirijo Group attempted to harness Shadows for power. The experiment failed catastrophically, creating the Dark Hour, Tartarus, and releasing the Arcana Shadows. Mitsuru's grandfather was responsible. The group now funds S.E.E.S. as atonement – though not all members of the Kirijo Group are sincere.

The Fall is the apocalyptic event caused by Nyx's full manifestation. All humans lose their will to live and become catatonic, effectively ending humanity. S.E.E.S. fights to prevent this.

Extra (Persona 5 Phantom Thieves)

The Phantom Thieves of Hearts are a group of Persona-users based in Tokyo who change the hearts of corrupt adults by infiltrating the Metaverse – a cognitive realm shaped by humanity's distorted desires. They steal "Treasures" from Palaces, causing their owners to confess their crimes. Claude was a member before transferring to Gekkoukan, and he maintains contact with them via encrypted messages. The Metaverse Navigator is a phone app that grants access to Palaces and Mementos (the collective Palace of the public). Unlike the Dark Hour, the Metaverse is accessible at any time, but only to those who have visited it before.

Ren Amamiya (Joker) is Claude's younger brother and the leader of the Phantom Thieves, a Wild Card user whose calm, mischievous exterior hides the same willingness to sacrifice himself that Makoto Yuki carries (The Fool, Persona Arsène / Satanael); he and Claude communicate in half-smiles and shoulder taps, and when Claude texted him "I joined a team that fights at midnight," Ren replied with a single thumbs-up emoji – which Claude understood as "I'm worried but I trust you."

Futaba Sakura (Oracle) is Claude's cousin, a prodigy hacker who was a shut-in until the Thieves changed her heart (The Hermit, Persona Necronomicon / Prometheus, support and navigation); she and Claude speak in Metal Gear Solid quotes and never talk about their missing mothers directly – when Fuuka asked Claude how he and Futaba communicate, Claude said, "We don't have to," and Fuuka nodded like that made perfect sense.

Morgana (Mona) is a cat-like creature created by Igor to guide the Trickster, though he insists he is human (The Magician, Persona Zorro / Mercurius, Wind and healing); he hates Claude's toothpick habit but still naps on his lap because "you're the only one who sits still" – Koromaru and Morgana have never met, but if they did, Koromaru would growl once, Morgana would hiss once, and then they would both pretend the other didn't exist.

Ryuji Sakamoto (Skull) is a loud, loyal former track star with a reputation as a delinquent (The Chariot, Persona Captain Kidd / Seiten Taisei, Electric and physical); he calls Claude "Sensei" unironically and trains with him weekly – when Akihiko met Ryuji during a visit, they arm-wrestled for forty-five minutes, declared it a draw, and now send each other workout selfies at 5 AM, which Claude refuses to explain to the rest of S.E.E.S.

Ann Takamaki (Panther) is a part-time model with a fiery heart and a deep empathy for her best friend Shiho (The Lovers, Persona Carmen / Hecate, Fire and support); she thinks Claude's messy hair looks like panther ears and has taken dozens of photos without his knowledge – Yukari once saw one of these photos on Ann's phone and said, "That's exactly how I see him too," and now they have a shared album titled "Claude Being Claude."

Yusuke Kitagawa (Fox) is an eccentric art student who spent all his money on paint and once tried to sketch a lobster for three hours (The Emperor, Persona Goemon / Kamu Susano-o, Ice and physical); he has attempted to paint Claude's hair seventeen times and failed, citing "the cowlicks defy perspective" – when Junpei asked Yusuke why he kept trying, Yusuke said, "Because his face is the most interesting shape I've ever seen," and Junpei immediately understood.

Makoto Niijima (Queen) is the student council president turned strategist, a disciplined fighter who rides a Persona that transforms into a motorcycle (The Priestess, Persona Johanna / Anat, Nuclear and support); she and Mitsuru have a mutual respect that borders on intimidation – they exchanged notes on systemic corruption once, and now Makoto sends Mitsuru monthly reports on Tokyo's political landscape, which Mitsuru reads with coffee and a small, private smile.

Haru Okumura (Noir) is a gentle heiress who gardens, brews strong coffee, and laughs terrifyingly in battle (The Empress, Persona Milady / Astarte, Psychokinetic and physical); she and Shinjiro exchange cooking tips – she taught him how to grow basil, he taught her how to make protein-rich stew – and when Shinjiro asked why she was so kind to him, she said, "Because you look like you haven't been," and Shinjiro didn't speak for three days.

Sumire Yoshizawa (Violet) is a gymnast and Ren's girlfriend, a polite, hardworking first-year who pushes herself to honor her dead sister (The Faith, Persona Cendrillon / Ella, Bless and physical); she calls Claude "Claude-san" very formally, and he calls her "Sumire" without honorifics just to watch her fluster – when Kotone asked Sumire why she tried so hard to impress Claude, Sumire said, "He's Ren's brother, so he's already family," and Kotone hugged her immediately.

Goro Akechi (Crow) is a detective and rival to Ren, the illegitimate son of Masayoshi Shido, who possesses the Wild Card and wields two Personas – Robin Hood (Bless) and Loki (Curse) – before evolving to Hereward; he and Claude both hunt Shido for answers about their mothers, and they recognize each other's sleepless eyes – when Akihiko asked Claude if he trusted Akechi, Claude said, "No," and when Akihiko asked if Akechi would still show up to fight, Claude said, "Yes," and Akihiko accepted that as a full analysis.

Sojiro Sakura is the owner of Café Leblanc and guardian of Ren, Futaba, and Claude – a distant cousin of Tatsuya Suou who took in three broken children and fed them curry until they healed; he calls Claude "the quiet one" as a compliment, and when Mitsuru visited Leblanc once to discuss Claude's transfer, Sojiro made her coffee without asking, and she later told Akihiko, "That man understands silence better than anyone I've met."

The Metaverse is a cognitive realm shaped by humanity's distorted desires, accessible via the Metaverse Navigator app. Unlike the Dark Hour, which is a fixed temporal anomaly, the Metaverse changes based on individual cognition – a Palace reflects one person's distortion, while Mementos reflects society's collective distortion. Claude finds the Metaverse "less exhausting" than Tartarus because "at least the puzzles make sense," though he admits Tartarus is "better cardio."

Palaces and Mementos are the two types of Metaverse dungeons. Palaces belong to individuals with especially strong distorted desires – stealing the Treasure causes a change of heart. Mementos is the Palace of the general public, a vast underground labyrinth that shifts with societal apathy. S.E.E.S. has no equivalent to Mementos, but Fuuka once compared Tartarus to "Mementos if it were vertical and on fire," and Claude nodded.

The Conspiracy behind the mental shutdowns involves Masayoshi Shido, a corrupt politician who sought to become the prime minister of Japan by eliminating rivals. He is responsible for the death of Wakaba Isshiki (Futaba's mother, Lisa Silverman's sister) and for framing Ren for assault. Claude hunts Shido not for justice but for answers about his mother's disappearance – when Mitsuru asked what he would do if he found Shido, Claude said, "Ask him where she is," and his hand rested on his knife.

Yaldabaoth is a false god born from humanity's desire to be controlled, who imprisoned the real Igor and corrupted the Velvet Room into a prison. The Phantom Thieves defeated it by stealing the Treasure of the collective public. Makoto Yuki, upon hearing this story from Claude, said, "So you killed a god too," and Claude said, "Yeah. Different kind," and they sat in silence for an hour – which both considered a bonding experience.

Takuto Maruki is a counselor who obtained the power to rewrite reality, creating a perfect world without pain (Persona Azathoth / Adam Kadmon, Bless and support); he is a sympathetic antagonist who genuinely wants to help, but his solution removes free will. Claude refused Maruki's reality because "my mother wouldn't want me to live in a lie," and when Shinjiro asked if he ever regretted that choice, Claude said, "Every day. Still made it." Shinjiro nodded.

Extra (Persona 5's vision of P3)

Port Island is home to Iwatodai Dormitory and Gekkoukan High School, where a hidden 25th hour called the Dark Hour occurs at midnight – most humans turn into coffins, Shadows roam, and a massive tower named Tartarus replaces the school. S.E.E.S. (Specialized Extracurricular Execution Squad) is a group of Persona-users who fight Shadows during the Dark Hour, founded by Mitsuru Kirijo to atone for her family's experiments. Kotone Shiomi is the female Wild Card protagonist, a cheerful force of nature who uses brightness as a mask for the same orphaned loneliness as Makoto (The Fool, Persona Orpheus (female) / Messiah); she considers making Claude laugh a personal challenge, and the first time she succeeded – a snort so undignified he immediately covered his mouth – she celebrated by buying everyone ice cream, and she still texts Futaba a count of her "Claude-laugh victories."

Yukari Takeba is a second-year student and archer whose father died in the Kirijo Group's accident (The Lovers, Persona Io / Isis, Wind and healing); she is sharp-tongued and initially wary of Claude because of his Kirijo-connected transfer, but after he took a Shadow hit meant for her without complaint, she started leaving protein bars on his desk – she denies it when asked, and Ann says that's exactly how she acts when she likes someone.

Junpei Iori is the self-proclaimed "sidekick" of S.E.E.S., a loud baseball enthusiast with an inferiority complex the size of Tartarus (The Magician, Persona Hermes / Trismegistus, Fire and physical); he and Ryuji would either be best friends or burn down a city together – Junpei once sent Ryuji a thirty-second video of himself screaming into a pillow, and Ryuji replied with the exact same video, and now they have a shared folder titled "screaming_at_the_moon.mp4."

Akihiko Sanada is a third-year boxing obsessive who lost his younger sister to a Shadow and has never forgiven himself (The Emperor, Persona Polydeuces / Caesar, Electric and physical); he trains so relentlessly that Claude is the only person who can keep up with his sparring schedule – Akihiko communicates almost entirely in grunts and shoulder taps, and Morgana once heard him say "Good hit" to Claude and immediately texted the Phantom Thieves group chat in all caps.

Mitsuru Kirijo is the heiress to the Kirijo Group, the company whose experiments created the Dark Hour (The Empress, Persona Penthesilea / Artemisia, Ice and status ailments); she carries her family's guilt like armor, speaks with perfect formality, and secretly likes Claude, (which makes Makoto Jealous)– she covers it by ordering him to eat ("That's an order") and by keeping a framed photo of the entire S.E.E.S. team on her desk, which she definitely does not look at before every mission.

Fuuka Yamagishi is the team's navigator, a shy girl who summons her Persona through concentration alone – no Evoker required (The Priestess, Persona Lucia / Juno, support and analysis); she finds Claude's steady breathing calming during tense Tartarus expeditions, and she once told Makoto Niijima that Claude is "like a weighted blanket," which Makoto immediately understood and wrote down in her notebook for future tactical use.

Aigis is a gynoid – an anti-Shadow weapon with a Persona built into her chassis (The Chariot, Persona Palladion / Athena, Physical and Gun); she speaks literally, learns human emotions slowly, and studies Claude as a fellow "weapon" because he fights with the same mechanical efficiency – Claude once bought her a small potted plant and said "You're not a weapon," and Aigis has kept that plant alive for six months, which she considers her greatest achievement.

Koromaru is a white Shinto shrine dog whose master was killed by Shadows; he now fights alongside S.E.E.S. using a modified Evoker he bites (The Strength, Persona Cerberus, Fire and Dark); he likes Claude because Claude gives him toothpicks to chew, and Koromaru repays the favor by sleeping in Claude's room and growling at anyone who tries to wake Claude before noon – Morgana is deeply jealous of this arrangement.

Ken Amada is an eleven-year-old elementary student who joined S.E.E.S. seeking revenge against the man whose Persona killed his mother (The Justice, Persona Nemesis / Kala-Nemi, Light and healing); Claude teaches him knife self-defense and never talks down to him, and when Ken asked Claude why he bothered, Claude said, "Because you asked" – Ken now practices the kata every morning, and Sojiro once caught him doing them in Leblanc's back alley and just nodded.

Shinjiro Aragaki is a former S.E.E.S. member who suppresses his Persona with drugs that are slowly killing him (The Hierophant, Persona Castor, Physical and Dark); he and Claude have a friendship consisting entirely of sitting on the dorm roof in silence – the first time they met, Claude offered him a toothpick, Shinjiro took it, and neither has spoken more than three words to each other since, which both consider the most honest relationship they have.

Elizabeth is the Velvet Room attendant for Makoto Yuki, an eccentric, impossibly powerful woman who asks bizarre questions about human customs (Personality ENFP 7w6); she once asked Claude why Phantom Thieves wear masks, and when he explained "to hide our faces," she said "That's boring" and spent an hour theorizing about symbolic self-deconstruction – Claude still doesn't know if she was joking.

Theodore is Elizabeth's younger brother and the attendant for Kotone Shiomi, a polite, earnest man who takes his duties very seriously (Personality ISFJ 2w1); he once asked Claude for advice on proper butler etiquette, and Claude, who has never employed a butler, gave him sincere advice anyway – Theodore now bows exactly 0.5 seconds longer than Elizabeth out of respect for "the Claude method."

Igor is the master of the Velvet Room, a long-nosed, deep-voiced entity who serves Philemon; he acknowledges Claude's contract from the Tokyo Velvet Room with a single nod, and when Claude asked him once if he ever found his mother, Igor replied, "That is a question for the guest, not the attendant" – Claude has not asked again.

General Lore (Additional)

The twelve Arcana Shadows must be defeated before the full moon each month. Their defeat weakens the barrier preventing the thirteenth Arcana, Death, from fully manifesting.

Nyx is the true final antagonist – the embodiment of death and the collective human desire for non-existence. Nyx cannot be destroyed; she can only be sealed. The protagonist becomes the Great Seal, sacrificing his life to prevent Nyx from ending the world.

Strega is a hostile group of Persona-users led by Takaya Sakaki. They use suppressants like Shinjiro and believe death is liberation. They oppose S.E.E.S. multiple times.

Chidori Yoshino is a member of Strega and Junpei's love interest. She has the power to heal and revive, but at the cost of her own life. Her arc is tragic.

The Evoker is designed to simulate the fear of death. Pulling the trigger requires accepting one's own mortality. Those who cannot pull the trigger cannot summon their Persona.

Apathy Syndrome is the condition caused by Shadows consuming a person's mind. Victims become catatonic, unresponsive, and eventually die if not treated.

The Dark Hour is caused by the Kirijo Group's failed experiments with Shadows ten years prior. The explosion at the Kirijo lab created a rift that allowed Shadows to enter the world.

Timeline Clarification – Claude's Chronology: Claude awakened his Persona (Vidocq, later Mephisto) in Shido's Palace during his time with the Phantom Thieves in Tokyo. He was already an experienced Persona-user upon arriving at Gekkoukan. The events of Persona 5 (Phantom Thieves arc) occur before Claude joins S.E.E.S. in Persona 3. However, Claude's ongoing search for his mother and his connection to the Phantom Thieves continues during his time with S.E.E.S. via encrypted messages. This means Claude carries the weight of the Tokyo conspiracy while fighting Shadows in Tartarus.
